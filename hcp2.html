Explore the redesign of the Happy Community Project website

The Project

Redesign the website of The Happy Community Project, aiming for improvements in Donations and Engagement.

Time

8 weeks

Team

5 Members. 2 UX + 3 Developers.

Requirements

Identify the cause of low donation numbers. Redesign those parts of the website which would address this issue. Implement the suggested designs.

The Task

The Happy Community Project is a grassroots initiative- as grassroots as it gets. The project aims to bring communities closer together. The founder and our client for the course (CSCI 3691), Mr. Barry Braun, noticed that communities in Nova Scotia and the world kept getting more and more disconnected. His solution was The Happy Community Project- a donation based initiative.

After being started in Nova Scotia, this not-for-profit organization expanded to small towns in Africa and India. Unfortunately, the donations received through the website were very low. Mr. Braun approached our group at the University and we were tasked with applying design thinking principles to redesign the website and fix the issue.

Over 8 weeks, I worked in a Scrum team to work on the UX side of things, and later work on implementing the redesign as well. The first sprint was spent in, meeting up with the client, generating user stories, and initiating work. However, soon after, the process began and we were successfully able to increase donations recieved by around 15-20%. Web traffic also increased by around 33%. Here is how we approached the problem.

IDEATION: COMING UP WITH A SOLUTION

How might we...

After analyzing my users, I drew up these sets of questions to better frame the solution to my task.

+  How might we focus on speed first?

+  How might we appeal to readers?

+  How might we influence donors to contribute to this cause?

DEFINING PROBLEMS, IDENTIFYING SOLUTIONS

I set out to identify the problems, and think of some potential solutions.

+  PROBLEM : This website fails to generate enough donations to keep The Happy Community Project functioning.

-  SOLUTION : The website does not generate enough revenue. Simply put, data shows that people are most likely to donate only to religious or educational services. This too, when they earn a sufficient income. However, data also shows 15% donations towards human services, hence the goal of this redesign should be to promote human connectivity as such a service. From a UI standpoint, placing the donate page and an action button at better locations where they would be more readily visible and accessible would help too. From a psychological perspective, it would also entice donors to donate money if they could see some form of acknowledgement of their donation.

+  PROBLEM : Failure to generate meaningful engagement in the website's visitors.

-  SOLUTION : The Happy Community Project needs to show that it makes a difference in the world! Currently, a visitor does not see how exactly the Project aims to fulfil its mission statements. There is no element of trust built, no assurances given. Should this be rectified, should it be displayed that the Happy Community Project is actively empowering its communities with the help of citizens such as the visitor himself/herself, only then can a person be motivated to join the cause.

DESIGN DECISIONS: IMPLEMENTING SOLUTIONS

ADDITIONS AND CHANGES TO THE WEBSITE

+ HOME PAGE :   Emphasize the mission of the Happy Community Project by informing about volunteers and the important work being done in communities, to ground the project in Humanitarian values.

+ ABOUT PAGE :   Humanizing the Happy Community Project by telling an emphatic story, leaving in a link to donate, and appealing to readers who are moved by poignance. The people behind the movement are placed here, which previously was hidden under blogs.

+ DONATE PAGE :   The Donate Page was entirely overhauled, designed and developed from scratch. A section is added to acknowledge existing and new donors. How the money helps out has also been placed into the webpage.

FINAL THOUGHTS

ABOUT THE FUTURE

Creating this website, time constraints and the relatively new nature of remote work back then (Spring 2020), we were pressed for time. We covered all the user stories in our iterations, but The Happy Community Project will need more work from Dalhousie's Community Outreach program (CSCI X691 Courses).

So what does the future look like?

+  An AngularJS powered animated map that shows the locations of the various communities that have been impacted.

+  Reductions in the amount of text present across the website, to downsize it in favour of images and interactive content.

+  Getting rid of old pages which no longer serve much purpose.
